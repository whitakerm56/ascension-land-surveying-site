Coastal NC & the Outer Banks

The easternmost stretch of our corridor, where flood elevation and shoreline change turn a boundary survey into something with real financial stakes for a property owner.

Coverage

Eastern NC through Dare & Currituck Counties

Coastal property brings a different set of survey needs than what we handle inland: FEMA elevation certificates that affect flood insurance premiums, waterfront boundary work complicated by shifting shoreline and riparian rights, and topographic surveys for construction that has to account for storm surge and setback requirements unique to the coast.

Drone LiDAR is especially useful here for capturing accurate elevation data across marsh, dune, and low-lying terrain that's slow and sometimes hazardous to traverse on foot.

Boundary surveys and plats here follow the standards set by the NC Board of Examiners for Engineers and Surveyors, and recorded subdivision plats are filed with the county Register of Deeds.
