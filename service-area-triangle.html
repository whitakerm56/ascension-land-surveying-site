NC Triangle Coverage

Land Surveying Services in the NC Triangle

The Research Triangle — anchored by Raleigh, Durham, and Chapel Hill and the universities and research institutions that define the region — has been one of the fastest-growing metro areas in the United States for the better part of two decades. That growth shows up directly in land survey demand: more commercial development requiring ALTA/NSPS surveys for lenders and title companies, more residential subdivisions requiring boundary surveys and subdivision platting, more construction activity requiring staking and as-built surveys, and more wooded land changing hands and requiring topographic surveys before development can begin.

Ascension Land Surveying holds North Carolina PLS License #L-5008 and serves the Triangle as part of our expanding NC coverage. From our base in Mocksville, the Triangle is our easternmost established NC region, and we're actively building out crew availability here alongside our Piedmont Triad home base. We take on Triangle projects now — reach out and we'll be direct about scheduling and current capacity.

Counties We Serve in the NC Triangle

  • Wake County — Raleigh and the surrounding municipalities including Cary, Apex, Holly Springs, Fuquay-Varina, Morrisville, and Garner. Wake County is North Carolina's most populous county and has one of the state's most active development markets. ALTA surveys for commercial acquisitions, topographic surveys for residential site development, and construction staking are all common project types. Plats record at the Wake County Register of Deeds.
  • Durham County — Durham and the Research Triangle Park corridor. Durham's urban core has active mixed-use and residential development driven by Duke University's surrounding neighborhoods and the broader RTP tech sector. Boundary surveys on older urban parcels with complex platting histories are common, as is commercial ALTA work along the major development corridors. Plats record at the Durham County Register of Deeds.
  • Orange County — Chapel Hill and Carrboro. Orange County's land development is shaped significantly by the presence of UNC-Chapel Hill and the county's active land conservation programs, which affect development density and parcel sizes in parts of the county. Boundary surveys, subdivision platting, and ALTA surveys are the primary project types. Plats record at the Orange County Register of Deeds.
  • Chatham County — Pittsboro and Siler City. Chatham County is experiencing development pressure from both the Triangle and the Triad sides, with large planned community developments and significant residential growth along the US-15-501 corridor. Topographic surveys and boundary surveys on large tracts are common project types as the county transitions from predominantly agricultural to mixed residential and commercial use.

Commercial Survey Demand in the Triangle

The Triangle's technology and life sciences economy generates a type of survey demand that differs from most of our other regions. Data centers, pharmaceutical manufacturing facilities, biotech campuses, and corporate headquarters — all of which have appeared in large numbers in Wake and Durham Counties — require ALTA/NSPS land title surveys as a standard part of commercial real estate acquisition. These surveys conform to the 2021 ALTA/NSPS Minimum Standard Detail Requirements and include boundary verification, easement and right-of-way location, and a range of optional items that most lenders require. Our team is familiar with ALTA survey requirements and has completed ALTA surveys for commercial projects throughout North Carolina.

NC Licensing and Recording Requirements

Land surveying in North Carolina requires licensure from the NC Board of Examiners for Engineers and Surveyors. Ascension Land Surveying holds NC PLS #L-5008. Boundary surveys and subdivision plats must be sealed with the PLS stamp and recorded at the appropriate county Register of Deeds. Wake, Durham, Orange, and Chatham Counties each have their own planning departments with specific requirements for subdivision plat review before recording. We coordinate that process as part of our delivery for subdivision platting projects.
