Services

Four ways we collect ground truth.

Every project starts with the same question: how accurate does this data need to be, and how fast do you need it? These are the methods we choose from.

01

Traditional Land Surveying

Boundary retracement surveys establish or confirm property lines using deed research, recovered monuments, and RTK GNSS or total-station measurement. We also handle ALTA/NSPS land title surveys, subdivision platting, and easement or right-of-way location.

This is the foundation every other survey product builds on — a title company, lender, or engineer relying on a plat needs to trust the boundary work underneath it, which is why it's a licensed, stamped deliverable in both NC and TN.

02

Drone LiDAR Surveying

Our FAA Part 107 certified drone platform carries a LiDAR sensor that penetrates tree canopy to capture ground-level elevation data — something aerial photogrammetry alone can't do in heavily wooded East Tennessee or Piedmont NC terrain.

A single flight can cover in hours what a ground crew would need days to traverse, which matters most on large acreage, steep terrain, or sites where safe ground access is limited.

03

3D Terrain & Topographic Mapping

Raw LiDAR point clouds are classified and processed into digital elevation models, contour maps, and topographic surveys delivered in the CAD or GIS format your engineering team already works in.

Used for site planning, drainage design, and any project where existing grade needs to be understood before design work starts.

04

Construction Staking & Monitoring

Once a project breaks ground, we provide layout staking to guide construction crews, plus as-built verification once work is complete. Repeat drone flights can track cut/fill progress across a site over time without shutting down active work.
