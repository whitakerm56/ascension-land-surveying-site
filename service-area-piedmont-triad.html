The NC Piedmont Triad: Where Our Survey Work Started

Ascension Land Surveying is based in Mocksville, Davie County, North Carolina — which puts us squarely at the center of the Piedmont Triad. We've been performing boundary surveys, topographic surveys, and subdivision platting across this region since 2012, and we have deeper familiarity with its recording offices, county GIS systems, and deed histories than any other territory we serve. When you hire a land surveyor in Winston-Salem, Greensboro, High Point, or any of the surrounding counties, you're working with a crew that already knows the local landscape.

The Piedmont Triad is one of the fastest-growing regions in North Carolina, and survey demand reflects that growth. Residential land development in Davie, Yadkin, and Iredell Counties is active. Commercial development around Winston-Salem and Greensboro drives steady ALTA/NSPS land title survey work for lenders and title companies. Engineering firms throughout Forsyth and Guilford Counties regularly commission topographic surveys and construction staking from our team. We're familiar with the local permitting environment and know how each county's planning department handles subdivision review.

Counties and Cities We Serve in the Piedmont Triad

Our Piedmont Triad service area covers the following counties:

  • Davie County — Home county and primary base of operations. Boundary surveys and residential subdivision platting make up a significant portion of our Davie County work. Plats are recorded at the Davie County Register of Deeds.
  • Forsyth County — Winston-Salem is Forsyth County's largest city and a major hub for commercial survey work. ALTA surveys for commercial real estate, topographic surveys for engineering projects, and boundary retracement surveys for residential and mixed-use development.
  • Guilford County — Greensboro, High Point, and the surrounding area. Guilford County has active development corridors along the I-85 and I-40 corridors where construction staking and topographic mapping are in steady demand.
  • Yadkin County — Rural agricultural and residential land. Boundary retracement work on older parcels where deed descriptions reference long-gone monuments is common in Yadkin County.
  • Rowan County — Salisbury and the surrounding area. Rowan County's industrial and residential development mix creates demand for both ALTA surveys and traditional boundary work.
  • Iredell County — Statesville and Mooresville. Iredell has seen strong residential growth driven by Charlotte metro expansion northward, creating demand for subdivision surveys and construction staking.
  • Surry County — Dobson, Elkin, and Mount Airy. Rural mountain-edge terrain where drone LiDAR is valuable for topographic surveys on wooded tracts.
  • Catawba County — Hickory and surrounding areas. Commercial and industrial survey demand in the Hickory metro corridor.
  • Caswell County — Rural central Piedmont. Agricultural and timber land boundary work, often involving older deed descriptions and historic plats.

Drone LiDAR Surveying in Piedmont NC

Piedmont North Carolina may not have the dramatic vertical terrain of the mountains, but it does have dense hardwood and pine cover on most undeveloped tracts — and that cover is exactly the condition where our drone LiDAR platform earns its keep. A conventional topographic survey of a wooded 50-acre parcel in Davie or Yadkin County might take a ground crew two or three days to traverse thoroughly. Our DJI Matrice LiDAR platform penetrates that canopy and produces a dense, classified point cloud in a single flight. For developers evaluating a site before acquisition, for engineers designing drainage for a wooded residential development, and for infrastructure planners mapping stream corridors, that's a meaningful reduction in both time and cost.

NC Recording and Licensing Requirements

Boundary surveys and subdivision plats in North Carolina must be performed by a Licensed Professional Land Surveyor, sealed with the PLS's stamp, and recorded at the county Register of Deeds. Ascension Land Surveying holds North Carolina PLS License #L-5008, originally issued May 14, 2012, and currently active. Survey work in the Piedmont Triad must meet the minimum standards set by the NC Board of Examiners for Engineers and Surveyors. Subdivision plats require county planning board review before recording in most Triad counties. We handle that coordination as part of our delivery process.
