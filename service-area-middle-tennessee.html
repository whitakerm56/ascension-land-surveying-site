Service Areas — Region 01

Middle Tennessee

Nashville and the fast-growing counties around it — where boundary work increasingly means untangling recent development against older rural plats.

Coverage

Davidson, Williamson, Rutherford, Wilson & Sumner Counties

Middle Tennessee has seen some of the fastest suburban growth in the state over the past decade, and it shows in the survey work: a lot of what we're called for here is boundary retracement on parcels where the original plat predates the subdivisions now surrounding it, plus ALTA/NSPS surveys for commercial development on the outskirts of Nashville proper.

The region's karst limestone geology — the same bedrock responsible for the area's sinkholes and cave systems — occasionally complicates both boundary monument recovery and site grading surveys, which is worth flagging early if your property sits in a known karst zone.

Tennessee boundary and platting standards are set by the Tennessee State Board of Examiners for Land Surveyors; recorded plats are filed at the county Register of Deeds.
