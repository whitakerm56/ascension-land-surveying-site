Land Surveying Services for Nashville & the Middle Tennessee Metro

Nashville and the surrounding Middle Tennessee counties have experienced some of the most intense real estate and commercial development activity in the southeastern United States over the past decade. That growth translates directly into survey demand — for boundary retracement on subdivided rural parcels, for ALTA/NSPS land title surveys required by lenders on commercial acquisitions, for topographic surveys ahead of site design, and for construction staking on active developments from Williamson County's suburban expansion to Davidson County's urban infill.

Ascension Land Surveying is licensed in Tennessee under PLS #3252 and actively serves the Middle Tennessee market. Counties We Serve in Middle Tennessee

  • Davidson County — Nashville proper. Urban and suburban boundary surveys, ALTA surveys for commercial development, and construction staking on infill and mixed-use projects throughout the county. The Davidson County Register of Deeds handles plat recording.
  • Williamson County — Franklin and Brentwood. One of the wealthiest counties in Tennessee, with active residential subdivision and commercial development. ALTA surveys and topographic surveys for development projects are in strong demand. Plats record at the Williamson County Register of Deeds.
  • Rutherford County — Murfreesboro and Smyrna. Rutherford County has seen particularly strong residential and industrial development driven by its location along the I-24 and I-840 corridors. Construction staking and subdivision platting are common project types.
  • Wilson County — Lebanon and Mount Juliet. Wilson County's growth is driven in part by its position between Nashville and the eastern suburbs, with strong residential development demand. Boundary surveys and subdivision plats are the predominant project types.
  • Sumner County — Hendersonville and Gallatin. Sumner County's suburban development north of Nashville creates consistent residential survey demand, including boundary surveys, topographic surveys for site grading, and construction staking.

What Types of Survey Work Are Most Common in Middle Tennessee

The Nashville market is commercially active in a way that drives specific survey types. ALTA/NSPS land title surveys are required by title companies and lenders on most commercial real estate transactions, and Nashville's commercial activity means these are a regular part of our Middle Tennessee workload. Topographic surveys commissioned by civil engineering firms ahead of site plan design are common across Williamson, Rutherford, and Davidson Counties. Boundary retracement work on older rural parcels in Wilson and Sumner Counties, where development pressure is encountering legacy agricultural plats with sometimes vague or conflicting deed descriptions, also generates consistent work.

A Note on Middle Tennessee's Geology

Middle Tennessee sits atop the Nashville Basin, which is underlain by karst limestone. This geology produces sinkholes, shallow bedrock outcrops, and cave systems across the region. For land development projects, these features can affect both site planning and the interpretation of topographic survey data. We flag karst-influenced terrain features when they appear in our survey work, as they can have direct implications for foundation design, drainage, and permitting.

TN Licensing and Recording

All surveying work in Tennessee is regulated by the Tennessee State Board of Examiners for Land Surveyors. Ascension Land Surveying holds TN PLS #3252. Plats in Middle Tennessee are recorded at the respective county Register of Deeds.
